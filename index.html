A Christmas Carol – overview

5 staves · main events · what Scrooge learns

Quick stave map

  • 1Marley’s Ghost – Scrooge is cold and miserly; Marley warns him to change.
  • 2Past – lonely childhood, Fezziwig’s party, Belle leaving.
  • 3Present – Cratchits, Tiny Tim, Fred’s party, Ignorance and Want.
  • 4Yet to Come – people steal his things; no one misses him; sees his grave.
  • 5Redemption – Scrooge wakes changed, helps others and becomes generous.

Key themes

  • ★Poverty & charity – attacks cruel ideas about the poor.
  • ★Responsibility – we owe care to others (“Mankind was my business!”).
  • ★Family & community – warmth of Fred & Cratchits vs Scrooge’s isolation.
  • ★Change & redemption – even Scrooge can completely change.
What does Scrooge learn in each stave? One quote per stave = basic paragraph.
“Marley was dead: to begin with.”
Confirms Marley’s death and sets up the ghost story.
“As solitary as an oyster.”
Scrooge is shut off but could have goodness inside.
“If they would rather die, they had better do it, and decrease the surplus population.”
Cruel view of the poor that Dickens criticises.
“Mankind was my business!”
Marley regrets not caring for others; warning to Scrooge.
“God bless us, every one!”
Tiny Tim – hopeful, kind, moral centre.
“I am not the man I was.”
Scrooge openly admits his change.
“He became as good a friend, as good a master, and as good a man.”
Narrator confirms Scrooge’s permanent redemption.

Language Paper 1

19th century extract + imaginative writing

Top-level plan

  • 1Start with Q5/6 (imaginative writing) – about 45 minutes.
  • 2Read the extract for around 10 minutes.
  • 3Answer Q1–4 with timings: Q1 2 mins, Q2 4 mins, Q3 10 mins, Q4 35 mins.

Q1–4 on the extract

  • Q1Comprehension – 1 mark · ~2 mins. Short answer using a quote or paraphrase.
  • Q2Inference – 2 marks · ~4 mins. 2 inferences with a quote or paraphrase each.
  • Q3Analysis – 6 marks · ~10 mins. Two small paragraphs: one language, one structure (Point → Evidence → Technique → Effect).
  • Q4Evaluation – 15 marks · ~35 mins. Two paragraphs using SPITTED and evaluative language like “the writer effectively creates…”.

Q5/6 – imaginative writing (40 marks · 45 mins)

  • DROPDescribe atmosphere (sensory + figurative language).
  • ZOOMZoom into the character in detail.
  • FLASHBACKWhy are they there? What led up to this moment?
  • ECHOReturn to present; echo or twist the opening image/line.

Checklist

  • VocabPrecise, interesting words.
  • Punctuation. , “ ” ! ? … - ; :
  • SentencesOne-word, adverb starter, dependent clause.
  • LanguageMetaphors, similes, personification, hyperbole, alliteration, onomatopoeia, repetition, juxtaposition, imagery.
  • StructureForeshadowing, withholding info, cyclical ending, pathetic fallacy.

Language Paper 2

Non-fiction + transactional writing

Top-level plan

  • 1Start with Q8/9 (transactional writing) – about 45 minutes.
  • 2Read Extract 1 · ~10 mins then Q1–3.
  • 3Read Extract 2 · ~10 mins then Q4–7a/7b.

Reading questions

  • Q1Comprehension – 2 marks · ~1 min.
  • Q2Comprehension – 2 marks · ~1 min.
  • Q3Analysis (Extract 1) – 15 marks · ~15 mins. Two paragraphs on language + structure.
  • Q4Comprehension (Extract 2) – 1 mark · ~1 min.
  • Q5Comprehension (Extract 2) – 1 mark · ~1 min.
  • Q6Evaluation (Extract 2) – 15 marks · ~15 mins. Two paragraphs using SPITTED and writer’s intentions.
  • Q7aSynthesis – 6 marks · ~6 mins. Three mini paragraphs: similarity + quote from each text.
  • Q7bComparison – 14 marks · ~15 mins. Two paragraphs comparing ideas + techniques.

Q8/9 – transactional writing (40 marks · 45 mins)

  • StatementMake your view clear.
  • CounterAcknowledge other side and show why it’s weaker.
  • AnecdoteShort story/example.
  • ReasonsStats / expert opinions.
  • Final pleaImperatives to push audience to act.

Literature Paper 1

Macbeth + An Inspector Calls

Total time: 1 hour 45 minutes.

  • Macbeth AExtract – 20 marks · ~30 mins.
  • Macbeth BWhole-play essay – 20 marks · ~30 mins.
  • AICEssay – 40 marks · ~45 mins.

Macbeth Part A – extract

  • FocusOnly the extract. No context.
  • ParagraphsAt least two. Point → Quote → Technique → Effect.

Look for: metaphors, blood as guilt, questions, soliloquies, asides.

Macbeth Part B – essay

  • FocusWhole play. Don’t reuse extract quotes.
  • Paragraph“Shakespeare presents…” → Quote → Explanation → Context → Shakespeare’s purpose.

Context: James I, witches, tragic heroes, Gunpowder Plot, Divine Right of Kings, Great Chain of Being, Jacobean gender roles.

An Inspector Calls

  • ChoiceTwo questions – answer one.
  • StructureIntro · 3 body paragraphs · conclusion.
  • Paragraph“Priestley presents…” → Quote → Explanation → Context (1912 vs 1945) → Priestley’s purpose.

Island Man – Grace Nichols tap ▾
About
A man wakes thinking he is in the Caribbean but is actually in London.
Key quotation
“his small emerald island” – metaphor makes the island precious and beautiful.
“dull north circular roar” – imagery makes London oppressive and overwhelming.
Structure & form
Free verse, no punctuation – dreamy flow between sleep and waking.
Context
Grace Nichols moved from the Caribbean to London; dual identity experience.
Themes
Youth, nature, nostalgia, dual identity, London as home.
To My Sister – William Wordsworth tap ▾
About
A man tells his sister to enjoy the power of nature in spring.
Key quotation
“Our minds shall drink at every pore the spirit of the season” – personification; nature gives them energy and refreshment.
Structure & form
ABAB rhyme, short stanzas – enthusiastic tone.
Context
Wordsworth was a Romantic: valued nature, youth, freedom, emotion.
Themes
Nature, family, exploration of self.
Sunday Dip – John Clare tap ▾
About
Boys enjoy their only day off by swimming in a river.
Key quotation
“And Laugh to hear the thunder in their ears” – metaphor; their noise is like thunder.
Structure & form
Repetition of “And” builds excitement and energy.
Context
Clare is a Romantic; simple form to be accessible.
Themes
Youth, nature, nostalgia, community.
I Remember, I Remember – Thomas Hood tap ▾
About
A chronically ill man remembers his childhood as joyful and perfect.
Key quotation
“My spirit flew in feathers then, / That is so heavy now” – metaphor; free and powerful when young, weighed down now.
Structure & form
Repetition of “I remember, I remember” shows deep nostalgia.
Context
Hood was actually chronically ill.
Themes
Youth, nature, nostalgia, exploration of self.
We Refugees – Benjamin Zephaniah tap ▾
About
Different refugees describe their experiences.
Key quotation
“We can all be refugees” – collective pronoun; it could happen to anyone.
Structure & form
Stanzas starting with “I” give different voices.
Context
Zephaniah is an activist for refugees and human rights.
Themes
Nostalgia, family, dual identity, community.
Jamaican British – James Berry tap ▾
About
A mixed-race speaker remembers bullying at school and later appreciates his Jamaican heritage.
Key quotation
“the English boys at school made me choose Jamaican, British?” – question mark + caesura show pressure and confusion.
Structure & form
Repetition of “Jamaican British” shows identity focus.
Context
Autobiographical – based on Berry’s experience.
Themes
Youth, family, dual identity, London as home, community, exploration of self.

  • ZOOM on character feelings.
  • FLASHBACK explains situation.
  • ECHO at end (cyclical feel).
  • Range of punctuation + sentences.

  • Counter argument handled.
  • Anecdote/example included.
  • Reasons with stats/experts.
  • DAFOREST used several times.

  • Short, relevant quotations.
  • Techniques + effects explained.
  • Context/purpose used when needed.
