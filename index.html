Language Paper 1 ‚Äì structure

19th-century fiction + imaginative writing

Order & timing

  • 1Start with Q5/6 (imaginative writing) ‚Äì about 45 minutes.
  • 2Read the 19th century extract for 10 minutes.

Questions 1‚Äì4 on the extract

  • Q2Inference ‚Äì 2 marks ¬∑ ~4 minutes. Make 2 inferences and support each with a quote or paraphrase.

Q5/6 ‚Äì imaginative writing (40 marks ¬∑ 45 minutes)

You can use the DROP / ZOOM / FLASHBACK / ECHO structure:

  • DROPDescribe the atmosphere using strong sensory language + figurative language.
  • ZOOMZoom into your character ‚Äì appearance, feelings, small details.
  • FLASHBACKShow why they are there; what led them to this moment.
  • ECHOBack to your character now ‚Äì what‚Äôs changed because of the flashback? Echo an earlier image/line.

What you must include

  • VocabStrong word choices (precise adjectives and verbs).
  • PunctuationUse a range: . , " " ! ? ‚Ä¶ - ; :.
  • SentencesInclude a one-word sentence, a sentence starting with an adverb, and a sentence with a dependent clause.
  • Language techniquesMetaphors, similes, personification, hyperbole, alliteration, onomatopoeia, repetition, juxtaposition, imagery.
  • StructureForeshadowing, withholding information, cyclical ending, pathetic fallacy.

Q8/9 ‚Äì transactional writing (40 marks ¬∑ 45 minutes)

Use this basic plan:

  • StatementMake your view clear straight away.
  • CounterAcknowledge the other side, then show why it‚Äôs wrong or weaker.
  • AnecdoteShort story or example linked to the topic.
  • ReasonsUse statistics or expert opinions to back you up.
  • Final pleaUse imperatives to push the audience to agree / act.

What you must include

  • VocabSame ‚Äúgood vocab‚Äù as Paper 1.
  • SentencesUse a one-word sentence, an adverb opener, and a sentence with a dependent clause.
  • DAFORESTDirect address, alliteration, fact, opinion, rhetorical questions, emotive language, statistics, tripling.
